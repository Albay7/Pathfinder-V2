CHAPTER I  
INTRODUCTION  
The purpose of this project is to design, develop, and evaluate  Pathfinder, an intelligent 
career guidance platform. The core mission of Pathfinder is to empower college students by 
providing them with personalized, actionable, and contextually relevant ca reer recommendations. 
This is achieved by synthesizing a holistic student profile ΓÇöencompassing their psychological 
disposition, self -assessed skills, and academic interests ΓÇöwith the current realities of the 
Philippine labor market.  Pathfinder operates on t he principle that effective career guidance must 
tell a complete story about the student and their opportunities. To build this story, the system first 
develops a deep understanding of the user through its dynamic assessment. A key part of this 
understandi ng is the student's  psychological profile, which is mapped using the  Myers -Briggs 
Type Indicator (MBTI)  framework. This framework helps categorize an individual's natural 
preferences in how they perceive the world and make decisions, resulting in one of 16  personality 
types (e.g., INTP, ESFJ). For instance, a profile might reveal a preference for Introversion (I), 
Intuition (N), Thinking (T), and Perceiving (P), indicating a personality that thrives in theoretical, 
flexible environments and excels at develo ping innovative models ΓÇöa natural fit for roles in 
research or strategic planning.  
This psychological insight is then woven together with the student's self -rated technical 
and soft skills, creating a multi -dimensional profile. Pathfinder's intelligence is driven by a  Hybrid 
Content -Based Recommendation Algorithm. This algorithm acts as the engine of the platform, 
intelligently matching the user's unique profile to potential career paths. It works by analyzing the 
semantic meaning of job descriptions and required skills using TF -IDF, calculating similarity 
scores based on the user's featu res, and employing a weighted ensemble method to synthesize 
these signals into a coherent and accurate list of recommendations.  
The final output is a comprehensive and intuitive career dashboard. Students are presented not 
just with a list of job titles, but with a clear narrative: a set of highly compatible careers, a visual 
breakdown of the rationale behind each suggestion, a det ailed skill gap analysis, and a curated 
learning path with direct links to resources designed to bridge those gaps. In essence, Pathfinder

1.1 PROJECT CONTEXT  
The growing demand for sophisticated career guidance solutions has spurred innovation in 
educational technology. However, many existing systems operate in silos: some focus on 
personality (e.g., platforms based on MBTI), others on skills, and most rely on international labor 
data irrelevant to the Philippine context. This results in recommendations that are often generic, 
non-actionable, or culturally misaligned. Pathfinder emerges to bridge these gaps by presenting a 
unified, holistic platform that integra tes MBTI personality assessment, self -rated skill inventories, 
and Philippine -specific labor market data through an intelligent Hybrid Content -Based Approach.  
Pathfinder is implemented as a full -stack web site using the Laravel  PHP framework, ensuring 
scalability, security, and a modern user experience. The system's context is defined by its key 
features:  
ΓÇó Dynamic Assessment System:  A branching questionnaire that adapts its subsequent 
questions based on the user's selected field of interest (9 colleges for courses, 9 industries 
for jobs), providing a highly personalized evaluation process.  
ΓÇó Comprehensive Career Planning Tools : Features like the Career Path Visualizer, which 
generates a step -by-step roadmap between roles, and the Skill Gap Analyzer, which 
identifies missing competencies and links directly to curated tutorials.  
ΓÇó Integrated Learning Management System (LMS):  A dedicated tutorial system that 
tracks user progress and provides recommended learning resources to address identified 
skill gaps.  
ΓÇó Explainable AI & Transparency:  The recommendations are accompanied by clear 
explanations, such as "Your INTP personality type, characterized by logical analysis and 
strategic thinking, combined with your proficiency in Python, shows a strong alignment 
with Data Scientist roles ΓÇ¥.

1.3 STATEMENT OF THE PROBLEM  
Choosing a career path is one of the most significant yet daunting decisions a college student 
faces. Many experience profound uncertainty, often exacerbated by traditional guidance systems 
that fall short. These systems frequently provide generic, one -size-fits-all advice that fails to 
reconcile a student's internal world ΓÇötheir unique personality, innate strengths, and passions ΓÇö
with the external landscape of job market demands and opportunities. This disconnect can lead to 
career indecision, choosing misal igned academic programs, and ultimately, professional 
dissatisfaction.  
Pathfinder is designed to address these critical gaps head -on by integrating a holistic view of 
the student with transparent, data -driven analysis. The study seeks to provide solutions by 
answering the following research questions:  
1. How accurately can the Pathfinder system's Hybrid Content -Based Algorithm generate 
career recommendations that align with the  studentΓÇÖs profile?  
2. How does  the inclusion of a psychological profile (MBTI) enhance the perceived 
relevance and user satisfaction of the career guidance provided?  
3. How does the explainable interface of Pathfinder, which details the rationale for each 
recommendation, impact user understanding and trust in the system?  
4. How does Pathfinder perform  in terms of providing information  compared to traditional 
career guidance methods?

1.4 HYPOTHESIS  
This study establishes that Pathfinder ΓÇöan intelligent career guidance system integrating 
Hybrid Content -Based Filtering with Myers -Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) profiling ΓÇöprovides 
significantly more accurate, relevant, and satisfying career recommendations for college students 
compared to traditional guidance methods. The findings reveal that the system's multi -faceted 
approach, combining semantic analysis of job descriptions (TF -IDF), feature similarity matching, 
and psychologically -informed personality ass essment, yields superior personalization and 
alignment with individual user profiles. Furthermore, the explainable AI components enhance 
user trust and decision -making confidence by providing transparent rationales for each 
recommendation.  
Empirically, Pathfinder demonstrates strong performance in generating culturally and 
contextually appropriate recommendations for the Philippine labor market by incorporating 
localized datasets and validated psychometric instruments. This integration enabl es the system to 
bridge the gap between student aspirations and market demands while addressing prevalent skill -
job mismatch issues. The platform's ability to provide actionable skill gap analysis and curated 
learning pathways further strengthens its pract ical utility in career development.  To validate 
these outcomes, the study employed a combination of precision -recall metrics, user satisfaction 
surveys, comparative analysis with single -algorithm systems, and expert evaluation by career 
guidance professionals. The evidence supports the conclusi on that Pathfinder represents a viable 
advancement in career guidance technology, offering benefits in both recommendation quality 
and user experience.  
The null hypothesis posited that there would be no significant difference in accuracy, 
relevance, or user satisfaction between the Hybrid CBF+MBTI system and traditional single -
algorithm approaches without personality assessment. It further assumed that th e inclusion of 
psychological profiling would not significantly enhance recommendation quality or user 
perception of the system's effectiveness.

1.5 RESEARCH OBJECTIVES  
This study is driven by the overarching goal of addressing the critical need for personalized, 
data-driven career guidance among college students in the Philippines. To systematically achieve 
this, the research is guided by the following general and specific objectives:  
1.5.1 General Objective  
To develop and evaluate the "Pathfinder: Intelligent Career Guidance System using a 
Hybrid Content -Based Algorithm and Myers -Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) for College 
Students."  
1.5.2 Specific Objectives  
a. To design and implement a dynamic assessment system that accurately captures a user's 
psychological profile (MBTI), skill proficiencies, and academic/career interests.  
b. To develop a robust Hybrid Content -Based Recommendation Algorithm that integrates 
TF-IDF, feature similarity, and weighted ensemble methods to generate personalized 
career and course recommendations.  
c. To evaluate the technical performance and accuracy of the recommendation algorithm 
against established benchmarks.  
d. To assess user perceptions regarding the platform's usability, the fairness of its 
recommendations, the clarity of its explanations, and overall satisfaction.

1.7 SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS  
The scope of this study is confined to the development and evaluation of Pathfinder, a Laravel -
based web application designed as an intelligent career guidance platform for Filipino college 
students. The system's core functionality encompasses user registr ation and profile management, 
dynamic branching assessments for both academic program and career recommendations, and a 
hybrid content -based recommendation algorithm integrating TF -IDF, feature similarity, and 
weighted ensemble methods. It utilizes the Mye rs-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) framework for 
psychometric profiling and includes advanced features such as a Career Path Visualizer for 
mapping progression between roles, a Skill Gap Analyzer that identifies competency deficiencies 
and links to curated le arning resources, and a personalized dashboard that tracks assessment 
history and tutorial progress. The platform is specifically calibrated with data relevant to academic 
programs courses in the Philippines and the Philippine job market.  
Several limitations are acknowledged within this defined scope. Firstly, the system's 
recommendation quality is inherently dependent on the integrity, comprehensiveness, and 
currentness of its underlying datasets for jobs, courses, and skills; as it does n ot incorporate real -

 This chapter discusses studies and scholarly works that support or highlight gaps related 
to the development of an intelligent career guidance system like Pathfinder. The discussion 
highlights four major areas: personality assessment using the Myers -Briggs  Type Indicator 
(MBTI), career guidance frameworks and digital platforms, recommender system methodologies 
with a focus on hybrid and content -based filtering approaches, and the integration of machine 
learning techniques in educational and career -oriente d applications. By examining these bodies 
of work, the chapter establishes the theoretical foundation for Pathfinder and identifies key 
research gaps. Specifically, the review emphasizes how personality -driven recommendations, 
when combined with hybrid rec ommender models, can enhance the accuracy and personalization 
of career guidance for college students navigating diverse academic and professional pathways.

college course  selection within Philippine universities. The system effectively matched student 
interests with course descriptions, highlighting the potential of content -based filtering in academic 
advising. However, the study did not incorporate personality profiling, such as the Myers -Briggs

3.1.1 DATA GATHERING  
 
Primary data for Pathfinder was sourced through a multi -faceted approach. Psychological 
profiling was conducted using the Myers -Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) framework, implemented 
via a dynamic, branching questionnaire that adapts based on the user's selec ted field of interest. 
This assesses an individual's preferences across four dichotomies: Introversion/Extraversion, 
Sensing/Intuition, Thinking/Feeling, and Judging/Perceiving, resulting in one of 16 distinct 
personality types. Skill assessments were cond ucted via user self -rated technical and soft 
competencies (e.g., programming, communication, leadership) tailored to specific career fields, 
measured on a Likert scale. To ensure continuous system improvement, user interaction data is 
dynamically collected  during platform engagement, including anonymized assessment answers 
and feature usage frequency.  
Secondary data integration incorporated comprehensive MBTI normative data obtained 
from reputable online psychological repositories, including the Open -Source Psychometrics 
Project, which provided a validated dataset of over 10,000 MBTI responses.  Data on academic 
programs from nine major collegiate fields and job descriptions from nine key Philippine 
industries were aggregated from official university publications and major job portals like 
JobStreet and LinkedIn. These datasets were preprocessed into structured formats, featuring 
attributes such as job titles, required skills, salary ranges, and personality -career alignment 
mappings (e.g., INTP for Data Science roles), ensuring recommendations were grounded in both  
psychological validity and local market realities.

ensemble method is used to combine multiple signals into a single, unified compatibility score. 
These signals typically include:  
1. The semantic similarity score from the TF -IDF/Cosine Similarity process.  
2. A similarity score based on the alignment between the user's MBTI type and the MBTI

required for a career.  
Each of these scores is assigned a specific weight based on its deemed importance to the 
final recommendation. The weights are determined during the algorithm's design and tuning 
phase. The scores are then combined, for example, through a weighted sum, to produce a final 
composite score for each career. The careers are ranked based on this final score, ensuring the 
recommendations are holistic, balanced, and context -aware.

3.6 RESPONDENTS OF THE STUDY   
 
The study engaged participants directly involved in the academic and career development 
ecosystem to ensure the Pathfinder system's recommendations are technically robust, relevant, 
and user -friendly. The primary respondents consisted of at least 20 - 50 Filipino college students 
recruited from any universi ty. These students represented a diverse range of academic disciplines 
and year levels to capture a wide variety of profiles and career planning needs.  
Their participation was crucial for evaluating the user experience (UX), interface usability, 
and the perceived relevance and accuracy of the career recommendations. Complementing this 
group, at least five (5) licensed guidance counselors or psychologist and career advisors were 
involved in the study. Their expertise provided professional validation of the assessment's 
psychological basis (MBTI) and the overall utility of the system as a counseling aid. Furthermore, 
at least five (5) industry professionals from various sectors in the Philippine job market were 
consulted to assess the alignment of the system's recommendations with real -world industry 
demands and trends.  
Data collection methods were multi -faceted. Students participated in user testing sessions 
where they completed the assessment and interacted with the recommendation dashboard; their 
interactions were anonymized and logged. Structured surveys and focus gro up discussions 
(FGDs) were conducted with all respondent groups to gather qualitative feedback on the system's 
explainability, fairness, and practical value. Ethical considerations were rigorously upheld. All 
participants provided informed consent after a detailed explanation of the study's objectives, data 
usage, and privacy protocols. All collected data was anonymized, and participation was voluntary, 
with the option to withdraw at any time without penalty.
